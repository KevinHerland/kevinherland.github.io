Notes / When It Works Best

This approach works best for rigid or semi-rigid objects that should not deform — such as helmets, armor plates, props, or mechanical parts bound to character joints. It accurately captures world-space motion and bakes it back to a clean transform.
If the mesh is highly deforming (soft skin, muscles), a single transform cannot perfectly represent its motion. In these cases, attach a locator to the surface or to a representative joint using a constraint/follicle/pointOnPoly, bake that locator’s motion, and then re-apply it to the object.

Pre-flight Checklist

  • Unparent or unbind the object if it is currently parented or skinned to a rig.
  • Make sure all transform channels are unlocked before running the tool.
  • Confirm the correct frame range and scene framerate.
